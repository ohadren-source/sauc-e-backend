## Abstract

This dissertation examines the epistemological claim "All entities certain of their correct certainty have died at the declaration" and its reflexive qualifier "Of that, I am certainly 99% certain," analyzing the multi-layered escape architectures embedded within this statement. Drawing from Platonic anamnesis, Groucho Marx's performative certainty, Dennis Reynolds' implicature theory, and quantum mechanical principles of superposition, we demonstrate that certainty functions as cognitive death while strategic uncertainty preserves epistemic vitality. Through phenomenological analysis of the adverb "certainly" (soytenlee), we reveal how pre-reflective understanding (3) precedes articulated thought (1) and formalized knowledge (2), ultimately collapsing into simultaneous tripartite cognition (312) upon axiom excavation.

## I. Introduction: The Certainty Paradox

The declaration under examination presents a recursive epistemological structure wherein the speaker claims that absolute certainty constitutes cognitive death, while simultaneously qualifying this claim with 99% certainty—thereby avoiding the very death sentence the statement describes. This self-demonstrating proposition operates across multiple semiotic registers, employing what we term "escape architectures": linguistic, logical, and phenomenological mechanisms that preserve superposition states while appearing to make definitive claims.

The statement reads in full:

> "All entities certain of their correct certainty have died at the declaration. Of that, I am certainly 99% certain. That being said, I have never encountered a scenario where 100% certainty did not = cognitive death. Ergo, I have always been right about this particular certainty. Now, I'm not saying that I know everything about certainty. Of course not. That would be hubris of the highest magnitude... It's just...ya know...THE IMPLICATION. ;)"

This work analyzes four primary escape mechanisms: (1) the 99% quantitative gap, (2) the adverbial qualifier "certainly," (3) the Groucho Marx performative tradition, and (4) the implicature framework derived from contemporary comedy theory.

## III. Escape Architecture One: The 99% Quantitative Gap

The claim "I am certainly 99% certain" establishes a 1% gap that functions analogously to Heisenberg's uncertainty principle. In quantum mechanics, position and momentum cannot be simultaneously known with absolute precision. Similarly, the speaker cannot claim absolute certainty (100%) without triggering the cognitive death described in the primary claim.

The 1% gap preserves epistemic "aliveness" through superposition—the simultaneous existence of multiple potential states prior to observational collapse. By refusing to declare 100% certainty, the speaker maintains a quantum superposition of epistemic states, preserving the capacity for becoming rather than static being.

### 3.2 Schrödinger's Epistemic Cat

Extending the quantum analogy, the speaker exists in a superposition state analogous to Schrödinger's cat: simultaneously certain and uncertain until the moment of final observation (deferred to "mort - 3," three units before death). This deferral of observational collapse preserves the dynamic process of optimization inherent in the question "How great can I become?"

To declare 100% certainty would collapse the superposition, rendering the speaker static and thus "dead" in the cognitive sense, unable to continue becoming.

## IV. Escape Architecture Two: The Adverbial Qualifier "Certainly"

### 4.1 Adverbs as Epistemic Hedges

The phrase "certainly 99% certain" contains a crucial adverbial modifier that functions as a secondary escape mechanism. Unlike the adjective "certain" or the noun "certainty," the adverb "certainly" introduces modal qualification and performative uncertainty.

When one says "certainly there must be oat milk at the store," the adverb signals:
- Incredulous expectation (I expect this to be true)  
- Complete openness to surprise (I am prepared to be wrong)  
- Questioning approximation (this is my best current assessment)

The adverb "certainly" thus undermines absolute certainty even as it appears to reinforce it—a performative contradiction that preserves epistemic flexibility.

### 4.2 The Groucho Marx Tradition: "Soytenlee"

The pronunciation "soytenlee" (certainly) references Groucho Marx's comedic tradition of performative certainty—asserting confidence through exaggerated formality while simultaneously undermining that confidence through tone and delivery. Groucho's "Certainly!" was always accompanied by an implicit wink, signaling that the speaker was performing certainty rather than embodying it.

This performative dimension adds a third layer of escape: not only is the certainty quantitatively limited (99%) and modally qualified (adverbial), but it is also performatively enacted rather than sincerely asserted.

- 100% empirical track record ("I have never encountered a scenario where 100% certainty did not = cognitive death")  
- Logical conclusion ("Ergo, I have always been right about this particular certainty")

### 5.2 The Functional-Technical Distinction

This creates a dual epistemic state:
- **Functionally certain** (100% track record, never wrong, implication suggests absolute correctness)  
- **Technically uncertain** (claims only 99%, hedges with adverbial qualifier, performs rather than asserts)

The speaker thus achieves the rhetorical force of certainty while avoiding its cognitive death through technical loopholes—what we term "epistemic escape velocity."

### 9.2 Wittgenstein's "On Certainty"

Wittgenstein's posthumous work examines the foundations of certainty, arguing that some propositions function as "hinges" upon which other knowledge turns—accepted not through evidence but as preconditions for epistemology itself. The certainty riff operates meta-epistemologically, examining the hinge propositions themselves and concluding that absolute certainty in any hinge produces systemic death.

### 9.3 Gödel's Incompleteness Theorems

Gödel demonstrated that any sufficiently complex formal system cannot be both complete and consistent—there will always be true statements that cannot be proven within the system. The 99% certainty functions analogously: the 1% gap represents necessary incompleteness, the acknowledgment that no epistemic system can achieve total closure without contradiction or undecidability.
