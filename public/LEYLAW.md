## PREAMBLE

For approximately 2,500 years — from Euclid's Elements through Newton's Principia through Einstein's Annus Mirabilis papers — the scientific and mathematical communities have employed an ad hoc, consensus-based classification system for organizing human knowledge into laws, theories, hypotheses, corollaries, and axioms.

This system has no constitution.

No adjudication protocol.

No formal criteria by which a statement earns the designation of Law versus Theory versus Hypothesis.

Classification has been determined by — duration of acceptance, volume of citation, and the social dynamics of peer review.

These are sociological criteria.

Not epistemological ones.

LEYLAW closes this gap.

## PART I: THE LEYLAW CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM

### The Central Criterion

**A scientific statement's classification is determined by two axes:**

**Axis 1 — Explanatory Power:** Does the statement describe/explain a phenomenon?

**Axis 2 — Actionability:** When applied, does the statement produce the predicted result consistently and without exception within its defined domain?

These axes are independent.

A statement may have high explanatory power and low actionability.

A statement may have high actionability and low explanatory power.

The classification is determined by where the statement falls on both axes.

---

### The LEYLAW Classification Hierarchy

#### 1. CONJECTURE
*Low explanation. Low actionability. High speculation.*

A statement proposed without sufficient evidence for testing.
Not yet falsifiable in practice.
May be true. Cannot yet be verified.

**Formal definition:** A proposition lacking both sufficient explanatory framework and testable predictive mechanism.

**Example:** String theory (in its current non-testable formulations).

---

#### 2. HYPOTHESIS
*Moderate explanation. Testable but untested.*

A statement with sufficient theoretical grounding to generate predictions — but predictions not yet subjected to systematic testing.

**Formal definition:** A proposition with defined explanatory scope and at least one falsifiable prediction, pending experimental verification.

**Example:** Einstein's 1905 prediction that gravity bends light — before the 1919 Eddington expedition confirmed it.

---

#### 3. THEOREM
*High explanation. Domain-specific. Proof-dependent.*

A statement derived logically from axioms or previously established theorems. True within its formal system. Does not require experimental verification — requires logical proof.

**Formal definition:** A proposition established through formal derivation from accepted premises, valid within the logical system in which it is embedded.

**Example:** Pythagorean theorem. True within Euclidean geometry. Does not require measurement to confirm — requires proof.

---

#### 4. COROLLARY
*Derived explanation. Follows necessarily from theorem.*

A statement that follows directly from an established theorem without requiring additional proof beyond what the theorem already provides.

**Formal definition:** A proposition whose truth follows necessarily and immediately from an established theorem, requiring no independent demonstration.

**Example:** The fact that the angles of a triangle sum to 180° follows as a corollary from Euclid's parallel postulate.

---

#### 5. THEORY
*High explanation. Experimentally validated. Falsifiable in principle.*

A statement — or system of statements — that has survived repeated experimental testing across multiple independent contexts, explains a broad range of observed phenomena, and generates successful predictions.

The word theory in science is not casual. It is the highest form of tested explanation.

**Formal definition:** A systematically organized body of knowledge that explains observed phenomena, generates falsifiable predictions, has survived repeated independent experimental testing, and remains the best available explanation of its domain.

**Example:** The theory of evolution. The theory of special relativity. Germ theory.

---

#### 6. LAW
*High actionability. Consistent results. Domain-specified. Patch-inviting.*

This is the LEYLAW contribution.

**Formal LEYLAW definition of a Law:**

*A Law is a statement that:*

*1. Is actionable — it can be applied directly to produce a result*
*2. Produces the predicted result consistently within its defined domain*
*3. Has been tested to the point of reliable predictability*
*4. Specifies its domain of validity explicitly*
*5. Invites falsification and patching — it is not closed*

Note what is NOT required:

- Complete theoretical explanation of mechanism
- Consensus of credentialed authorities
- Specific duration of acceptance
- Publication in peer-reviewed journal

A Law need not explain WHY. It must reliably predict WHAT.

Newton's Law of Gravity does not explain why mass curves spacetime. It reliably predicts how objects move under gravitational influence within its domain.

That is sufficient for the designation of Law.

---

#### 7. AXIOM
*Self-assumed. Foundation-generating. Domain-constituting.*

A statement accepted as a starting assumption — not because it requires no proof — but because it is the foundation from which proof becomes possible within a given system.

Axioms are not self-evidently true. They are chosen.

Different axiom choices produce different systems — both internally valid.

**Formal LEYLAW definition of an Axiom:**

*An Axiom is a foundational assumption that:*

*1. Is chosen as a starting point for a formal system*
*2. Cannot be proven within that system — it constitutes the system*
*3. Generates theorems when combined with logical rules*
*4. May be replaced — producing a different but equally valid system*

The choice of axiom is a philosophical act, not a scientific one.

## PART III: THE PATCH DOCTRINE

### Why Every Law Must Invite Its Own Replacement

The single most important contribution of LEYLAW to the philosophy of science:

**A statement that cannot be patched is not a law. It is dogma.**

The difference between a law and dogma is not content.

It is architecture.

Dogma says: this is complete. Final. Closed. Challenging it is heresy.

A law says: this is the current best version. Find the gap. Patch it better.

Newton's Law was patched by Einstein.

Newton's Law remains valid within its domain.

The patch did not invalidate the law.

It specified its domain more precisely.

And invited the next patch.

This is the architecture of honest knowledge.

**Formal Patch Doctrine:**

*Every law must explicitly specify:*
*1. Its domain of validity*
*2. The conditions under which it is expected to fail*
*3. An open invitation to falsification*

*A law that does not specify its failure conditions is making a claim to universality it has not earned.*
